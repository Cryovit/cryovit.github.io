Majsoul Rank System

Introduction

  • Majsoul uses a ladder system to rank players. You starts from the lowest rank, gaining points by performing well in matches, and promote to higher ranks when you have enough points. You will also lose points and demote. Every match is considered separate and the point changes are only based on the match results.
  • There are three servers: China (operated by Catfood), Japan (operated by Yostar), and Global (operated by Yostar). Rank and leaderboard are separate for each server, even though you can match with players from other servers.
  • There are two ranks for every player: 4P (4-player) and 3P (3-player). The two ranks are independent of each other, with same ranking system but different point calculation.

Rooms

There are five levels of ranked rooms. Within each level, you can choose to play E (east-only) or S (east-south) rounds.

You can only enter rooms corresponding to your rank. For example, if you are a 4P Saint and 3P Expert, you can enter 4P Jade or Throne room, and 3P Silver or Gold room, but not any other rooms.

Bronze Novice - Adept 10/5/0/- 20/10/0/- 15/0/- 30/0/-
Silver Adept - Expert 20/10/0/- 40/20/0/- 30/0/- 60/0/-

The last four columns denote the point changes for 1st/2nd/3rd/4th place. This is not the only factor that determines the point changes. Please refer to the Calculation section for more details.

Ranks

There are 6 main ranks, from lowest to highest:

  • Novice (初心)
  • Adept (雀士)
  • Expert (雀傑)
  • Master (雀豪)
  • Saint (雀聖)
  • Celestial (魂天)

Ranks from Novice to Saint each has 3 levels, numbered 1 to 3 from lowest to highest.

Celestial has a different level system, with up to 20 levels.

Details of the rank requirements are listed below:

The 4P 4th Penalty and 3P 3rd Penalty columns denote the point penalty for 4th place in 4P rooms and 3rd place in 3P rooms. That’s why the 4P 4th place point change and 3P 3rd place point change are ignored in the room table above - this is dependent on the rank, instead of the room.

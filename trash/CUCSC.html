CityU Ultimate Computer Science Challenge 2023

CityU Ultimate Computer Science Challenge (CUCSC) is a competition for undergraduate students, open to all students in Hong Kong. It is organized by the CityU CS Department, collaborating with CityU EE and Data Science Department, as well as PolyU COMP Department.

The competition features a series of challenges covering all aspects in computer science, including but not limited to: algorithms, data structures, programming, operating systems, computer networks, databases, machine learning, web security, and cryptography.

Participants should form a team of 2-3 members, and compete in the online preliminary round. The top 10 teams will be invited to the on-site final round, where they will compete for the championship.

Registration

Registration is open to all undergraduate students in Hong Kong. You must provide your Hong Kong ID, student ID, and email address.

Each team should have 2-3 members. You can register as a team, or as an individual. If you register as an individual, you will be randomly assigned to a team (with individuals from the same school if possible).

Registration is free, and will be open from 2023-02-01 to 2023-02-28.

Preliminary Round

  • Registration: 2023-02-01 to 2023-02-28
  • Date: 2023-03-04 09:00 to 2023-03-04 21:00 (12 hours)
  • Format: Online

Preliminary round features about 40 problems. The result will be ranked according to ICPC rules (i.e., first by number of problems solved, then by submission time).

All teams who have solved at least one problem will receive a certificate of participation. The top 10 teams will be invited to the on-site final round.

Since the round is online, cheating and unauthorized assistance are strictly prohibited. Any team found cheating will be disqualified.

Participants will only be allowed to communicate with their teammates, or ask for clarification from the organizers, on a Discord server. (Participants from the same team will be invited to a private channel so the other teams cannot see their messages, but admins can still see them. Clarification can be asked in public or private channels.) Any other form of communication is prohibited.

On the other hand, refering to online resources is allowed. However, you should not copy code from other sources, and anti-plagiarism tools will be used to detect cheating.

Final Round

  • Date: 2023-03-11 09:00 to 2023-03-11 21:00 (12 hours)
  • Format: On-site
  • Venue: CityU Computer Lab

Final round features about 10 problems. The result will be ranked according to ICPC rules (i.e., first by number of problems solved, then by submission time).

Each team will be assigned three computers in the lab, pre-installed with Ubuntu 20.04 and some third-party software (refer to official rules for details). You can bring your own keyboard, mouse, and headphones, but no other electronic devices are allowed.

Participants can leave for lunch and dinner during given break times (11:30-14:00 and 17:00-19:30). However, here are a few rules to prevent cheating:

  • At least one member of each team must stay in the lab at all times.
  • Each member can only leave the lab once for no more than 60 minutes each break. (Care for your teammates - don’t stay out for too long!)
  • Leave and return will be recorded by the organizers.

Prizes

  • Champion: HKD 8,192 + trophy + medals + certificates
  • 1st runner-up: HKD 4,096 + medals + certificates
  • 2nd runner-up: HKD 2,048 + medals + certificates
  • All other finalists: HKD 512 + certificates

Note: There is only one trophy for Champion. For medals and certificates, each team member will receive one.

  • Preliminary round top 20%: certificate of excellence
  • Preliminary round top 50%: certificate of merit
  • Preliminary round, at least one problem solved: certificate of participation

Introduction

OCET is a web-based English test system, set up by the College English Teaching Advisory Board of the Ministry of Education of China, and developed by the National Education Examinations Authority. The system was developed in 2020 in response to the COVID-19 pandemic, and is used by students in China to take the College English Test (CET) online.

OCET can also refer to the test itself. The test is a computer-based test, and is taken by university students in China. The test is used to assess the English proficiency of students, and is a requirement for graduation. The test is administered by the National Education Examinations Authority, and is held twice a year, in June and December.

Despite passing OCET-4 and OCET-6 can also fulfill the English requirement for graduation, the test differs from traditional CET-4 and CET-6 in that the question types and paper structure are completely redesigned, and the total score and passing score are also adjusted. The test is also administered online, and the test questions are generated randomly by the system.

Paper Structure

The test consists of 4 sections, and the total score is 100 points. The passing score is 60 points.

The time limit for the test is 125 minutes, and the timer can be seen on the top right corner of the screen.

The sections are as follows:

  • Section 1: Listening Comprehension (30 points, about 25 minutes)
  • Section 2: Reading Comprehension (30 points, about 40 minutes)
  • Section 3: Applied Writing (15 points, about 20 minutes)
  • Section 4: Integrated Writing (25 points, about 40 minutes)

There are no time limits for each section, and the candidate can choose to take the sections in any order. However, the candidate must complete all sections within the time limit.

As a comparison, the traditional CET-4 and CET-6 tests consist of 4 sections, the time limit is also 125 minutes, and the passing score is 425 out of 710 points. Its paper structure is as follows:

  • Section 1: Applied Writing (15%, about 30 minutes)
  • Section 2: Listening Comprehension (35%, about 25 minutes)
  • Section 3: Reading Comprehension (35%, about 40 minutes)
  • Section 4: Translation (15%, about 30 minutes)

Test Environment

The test is taken on the open-source Safe Exam Browser (SEB) platform, which is a web browser that restricts the candidate from accessing other websites or applications during the test. The candidate can only access the test website, and the test website will be opened in full-screen mode.

During the test, the front camera of the candidate’s computer will be turned on, and the candidate’s face will be recorded. The candidate’s face will be compared with the photo on the candidate’s ID card, and the candidate’s ID card will be scanned to verify the candidate’s identity.

The candidate can take notes or draft on a maximum of 5 sheets of A4 paper, which will be examined before and after the test. The candidate can also use a pen and a pencil during the test.

Question Types

Listening Comprehension

The candidate should test microphone and speaker before the test starts.

The recording for each dialogue or monologue will be only played once, and cannot be paused once it starts. The questions are given after dialogue or monologue ends, and only the choices are given on the screen. The candidate should choose the correct answer and click the “Next” button to go to the next question.

Short Dialogues

  • Format: Multiple choice
  • Dialogues: 7
  • Questions: 7
  • Points per question: 1
  • Total points: 7

Long Dialogues

  • Format: Multiple choice
  • Dialogues: 3
  • Questions: 9
  • Points per question: 1
  • Total points: 9

Monologues

  • Format: Blank filling
  • Monologues: 3
  • Questions: 14
  • Points per question: 1
  • Total points: 14

For each test, all the candidates will be given the same dialogues and monologues. But within Short Dialogues, Long Dialogues, and Monologues, the order of the listening materials will be randomized for each candidate.

Reading Comprehension

Short Passages

CET-4 passages is about 350 words, and CET-6 passages is about 500 words.

  • Format: Multiple choice
  • Passages: 3
  • Questions: 10
  • Points per question: 1.5
  • Total points: 15

For each test, NEAA will prepare 2 short passages for type A, B, and C, respectively, and for each type, the candidate will be given one of the two passages randomly. Type A and B are followed by 3 questions, and type C is followed by 4 questions.

Vocabulary Fillings

The passage is about 600 words. There will be 10 blanks in the passage, and candidates will be given 15 words as choices for all blanks. The candidate should choose one from the 15 words and fill its correct form into the blank.

  • Format: Blank filling
  • Passages: 1
  • Questions: 10
  • Points per question: 0.5
  • Total points: 5

For each test, NEAA will prepare 2 vocabulary fillings passages, and the candidate will be given one of the two passages randomly.

Comprehensive Matching

The passage is about 1500 words. There will be 10 blanks in the passage, and candidates will be given 15 sentences as choices for all blanks. The candidate should choose one from the 15 sentences and fill its choice letter into the blank.

  • Format: Blank filling (choice)
  • Passages: 1
  • Questions: 10
  • Points per question: 1
  • Total points: 10

For each test, NEAA will prepare 2 comprehensive matching passages, and the candidate will be given one of the two passages randomly.

Applied Writing

The candidate should write an essay of about 150 words on a given topic. The candidate should write the essay in the text box on the screen, and the candidate can use the spell check function.

  • Words Requirements (Official): 150 words
  • Words Limits (Suggested): 150-200 words
  • Total points: 15

For each test, NEEA will prepare two topics, and the candidate will be given one of the two topics randomly.

Integrated Writing

This can be one of the following types:

  • Continued Writing: The candidate should read a story of about 500-600 words, and write a continuation of about 200 words in two paragraphs. The first sentence of both paragraphs is given, and the candidate should write the rest of the paragraphs in the text box on the screen. The candidate can use the spell check function.
    • Words Requirements (Official): 200 words
    • Words Limits (Suggested): 100-150 words per paragraph
    • Total points: 25
  • Outlined Writing: The candidate should read a report, essay or other non-fictional article of about 800-1000 words, and write an outline of about 150 words in three paragraphs. The candidate should write the outline in the text box on the screen, and the candidate can use the spell check function.
    • Words Requirements (Official): 150 words
    • Words Limits (Suggested): 130-160 words, never go beyond the upper limit
    • Total points: 25

For each test, NEEA will prepare one Continued Writing and one Outlined Writing, and the candidate will be only presented with one of them, randomly chosen by the system. Candidate should prepare well for both types of Integrated Writing.

After the test, all the prepared questions will be published on the official website of NEEA, given in the original order.

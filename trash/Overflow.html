Introduction

Overflow is a new mobile rhythm game developed by PixelTail Games.

There are three main difficulties in Rizline: INF (Infinity), ETN (Eternity), and REL (Reality).

There is an additional difficulty, IMG (Imaginary), which is only available for some songs and requires special unlock conditions.

Chart Constant

Each chart has an internal constant to specify the difficulty, known as chart constant or CC.

The integer part of the CC is the difficulty level, and the fractional part is not displayed in-game.

Additionally, the difficulty level limits the possible value of fractional part:

  • For level 1-7, the fractional part can be 0.0 or 0.5.
  • For level 8-10, the fractional part can be 0.0, 0.2, 0.5, or 0.7.
  • For level 11-13, the fractional part can be any value between 0.0 and 0.9, with a step of 0.1.
  • For level 14-16, it is same as level 11-13, but when the fractional part is at least 0.7, the level is displayed as 14+, 15+, or 16+.

Overflow

Overflow (OF) is a value that represents the capability of a player based on his best and recent performances.

Player OF formula is: OF = (best30 + recent10) / 40.

Whereas best30 is the sum OF for the 30 best scores, and recent10 is the sum OF for the 10 best scores selected from the most recent 30 plays.

For each play, OF formula is: OF = floor(power(CC * 10, score)).

Whereas score is the score percentage, and power means exponentiation.

Furthermore, some bouns will be added to the OF for each play:

  • +1 for Full Connection (FC).
  • +1 for All Precision (AP).
  • +1 for score percentage reaches X (100%)
  • +1 for score percentage reaches M (101%)

Score

The score is simply the accuracy of the play. The connections (meaning the number of notes hit in a row) are not taken into account. (But the connections are still displayed in-game, used for some challenges, and used for the OF calculation.)

The accuracy value is calculated as follows:

Judgment Timing Accuracy
Max Precise (MP or MAX) -20~20ms 101%
Precise (P) -40~40ms 100%
Connect (C) -80~80ms 50%
Lost (L) -100~-80ms or 80ms+ 0%

The score is given a letter grade based on the accuracy:

Grade Accuracy
M 101%
X++ 100.75%
X+ 100.50%
X 100.00%
A++ 99.50%
A+ 99.00%
A 98.00%
B++ 97.00%
B+ 96.00%
B 95.00%
C++ 92.50%
C+ 90.00%
C 85.00%
L 0.00%

Notes:

  • When score reaches 101.00%, it is displayed as 101% instead of 101.00%.
  • When score is AP and at least X++, the percentage is replaced by (-X) where X is the non-Max Precise count. These are to distinguish the score from non-AP X++ and M.
  • The letter grade is pretty much a legacy from the v1.x days. It is still used in the game, but it is not used in the OF calculation. (During v1.x, the OF formula changes at 100.00%, 98.00% and 95.00%, reaches minimum at 85.00% and maximum at 100.75%. Today the OF formula is continuous and still work below 87.50% or above 100.75%.)

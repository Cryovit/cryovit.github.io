CityU Graduation Q&A 2023

Read Before Proceeding

The content of this article is purely fictional and is not related to any organisation or individual in real life.

In addition, it contains GPT-3.5 generated text, which may not be accurate or reliable. Please read with caution.

This article was created on 2023-05-07. Have fun reading!

Q: What is Graduation Date for 2023?

A:

  • For students graudate in Semester A, the graduation date is 1 Feb 2023.
  • For students graudate in Semester B, the graduation date is 1 Jun 2023.
  • For students graudate in Summer Term, the graduation date is 1 Oct 2023.

Q: What is the difference between Graduation Date and Graduation Ceremony?

A: Graduation Date is the date that you are officially graduated from CityU. Graduation Ceremony is the date that you attend the ceremony and receive the certificate.

There are three graduation dates in a year, each corresponding to a term.

However, since most students graduate in Semester B, there is only one graduation ceremony in a year, which is held in mid-May.

After your graduation date, your student status will be changed to “Graduated” (for public access) or “Alumni” (for internal access). This means you

  • are no longer a student
  • cannot receive any student benefits
  • cannot enter the campus without permission

You should check your credit card, visa, and other documents since they may be expired after your graduation date.

You can check your graduation date in AIMS.

Q: What if I continue to further study in CityU?

A: You will be considered as a new student and you will have a new student ID. You will not be able to use your old student ID to access the campus.

And in the gap between your graduation date and the start of your new study, you are still not considered as a student. You can, however, access campus as Alumni (which costs HKD 100 per year).

Q: I am going to exchange in Year 4 Semester B. Will this affect my graduation date?

A: Yes. You will need to present your final year project in Summer Term. All other courses planned in Semester B will be exempted (unless otherwise specified by your department). Your graduation date will thus postpone to 1 Oct 2023.

You may or may not be able to attend the graduation ceremony in May, depending on your exchange period. But you can join the ceremony online (via Zoom) if you cannot attend in person.

Q: Further on last question, will this affect my postgraduate application?

A: Yes, to some extent. You can only apply for CityU postgraduate programmes in the year you graduate. For other universities, you will need to apply in the next year.

You should take serious consideration on this. If you are planning to apply for postgraduate programmes in other universities, you should not go for exchange in Year 4 Semester B.

Q: So Year 4 Semester B is my last semester?

A: Yes, generally. If you fulfill all the requirements, you will graduate in Semester B. You cannot take any courses in Summer Term or participate in university programmes in Summer Term since you are no longer a student. However, if you have academic standing issues, you may need to take courses in Summer Term to fulfill the requirements.

Q: Can I graduate in Year 4 Semester A if I fulfill all the requirements?

A: Yes, you can. But you will need to apply for early graduation. You can apply for early graduation in Year 3 Semester B. The application deadline is usually in late March. You can check the details in CityU website.

You will not be able to attend the graduation ceremony in May in person. But you can join the ceremony online (via Zoom).

Q: Can I graduate in Year 3 or even earlier?

A: No. The maximum credits per semester is designed to prevent you from doing so.

Q: Can I postpone my graduation date?

A: No, generally. Once you fulfill all the requirements, you will graduate in the corresponding semester.

The only exceptions have been mentioned above:

  • Exchange in Year 4 Semester B, which will postpone your graduation date to Summer Term.
  • Not applying early graduation in Year 4 Semester A, which, if you wish, will let you graduate like other students in Semester B.

However, if you do have special reasons with strong justifications, you can apply for late graduation. You can check the details in CityU website.

Q: Can I attend the graduation ceremony in May if I graduate in Summer Term?

A: It depends:

If you cannot graduate in Semester B because of exchange, you can attend the graduation ceremony in May in person or via Zoom. But you will not receive the certificate in May. You will receive the certificate in Summer Term.

If you cannot graduate in Semester B because of academic standing issues, you cannot attend the graduation ceremony in May.
